Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Cleveland, Ohio

Chapter 7 is the most common form of bankruptcy. It eliminates most unsecured debts in 3-4 months. Cleveland residents file in the Northern District of Ohio.

What Is Chapter 7 Bankruptcy?

Chapter 7 bankruptcy, sometimes called "liquidation bankruptcy," eliminates most unsecured debts. A court-appointed trustee reviews your assets, but most Cleveland filers keep all of their property thanks to Ohio's exemptions.

After filing, you attend a 341 meeting of creditors at the N.D. Ohio courthouse. About 60 days later, you receive your discharge -- the order that eliminates your qualifying debts.

The Means Test in Ohio

To qualify for Chapter 7, you must pass the means test. This compares your household income to the Ohio median income:

  • If your income is below the Ohio median for your household size, you qualify automatically
  • If your income is above the median, a detailed calculation of your expenses determines eligibility
  • Social Security income is excluded from the means test calculation

What Debts Does Chapter 7 Eliminate?

Debts typically discharged:

  • Credit card debt
  • Medical bills
  • Personal loans
  • Utility bills
  • Old lease obligations

Debts generally NOT discharged:

  • Student loans (except in rare hardship cases)
  • Recent income taxes (last 3 years)
  • Child support and alimony
  • Debts from fraud or DUI
  • Government fines and penalties

Chapter 7 Timeline in Cleveland

  1. Credit counseling -- complete an approved course before filing
  2. File petition -- submit to the N.D. Ohio at 801 W Superior Ave, Cleveland 44113
  3. Automatic stay -- creditors must stop all collection activity immediately
  4. 341 meeting -- attend your meeting of creditors (~30 days after filing)
  5. Debtor education -- complete a second required course
  6. Discharge -- receive your discharge order (~60 days after 341 meeting)

Total time: approximately 3-4 months from filing to discharge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What debts does Chapter 7 eliminate in Cleveland?

Most unsecured debts including credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and utility bills. Student loans, recent taxes, child support, and alimony generally survive.

Do I qualify for Chapter 7?

You must pass the means test. If your household income is below the Ohio median, you automatically qualify. Learn about the means test.

How long does Chapter 7 take?

About 3-4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting happens about 30 days after filing.

Will I lose my car?

Ohio's vehicle exemption protects $4,060 of equity. If your equity is under that limit, you keep it. Learn more.
