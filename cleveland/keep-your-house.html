Keeping Your House in Bankruptcy - Cleveland, Ohio

Facing foreclosure or worried about losing your home? Ohio's generous homestead exemption and Chapter 13 protections help most Cleveland homeowners keep their homes.

Ohio's Homestead Exemption

Ohio protects $145,425 of equity in your primary residence. For married couples filing jointly, this doubles to $290,850.

  • Home worth $250,000, owe $180,000: Equity $70,000 - fully protected
  • Home worth $300,000, owe $200,000: Equity $100,000 - fully protected
  • Home worth $400,000, owe $200,000: Equity $200,000 - exceeds single filer exemption

Chapter 7 and Your Home

In Chapter 7, your home is protected if equity does not exceed the exemption. You must stay current on mortgage payments - Chapter 7 does not provide a way to catch up on arrears.

Chapter 13: Stop Foreclosure

  • The automatic stay stops foreclosure immediately
  • Missed mortgage payments spread over 3-5 years in your plan
  • Continue making regular mortgage payments going forward
  • Keep your home regardless of equity level
  • Second mortgages may be "stripped" if home is worth less than first mortgage

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep my house?

Usually yes. $145,425 homestead exemption protects most homeowners. Chapter 13 lets you catch up on payments.

Can bankruptcy stop foreclosure?

Yes. The automatic stay stops it immediately. Chapter 13 lets you cure arrears over 3-5 years.

What if my equity exceeds the exemption?

Consider Chapter 13 where you keep the home regardless. Or consult an attorney about federal exemptions.
