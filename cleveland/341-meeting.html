341 Meeting of Creditors -- Cleveland, Ohio

The 341 meeting is a required part of every bankruptcy case. It is shorter and less intimidating than most people expect.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting (also called the "meeting of creditors") is a brief hearing about 30 days after filing. A bankruptcy trustee -- not a judge -- asks questions under oath about your financial situation.

Detailed 341 meeting resource →

Where and When

Location: 801 W Superior Ave, Cleveland, OH 44113 (or by phone/video)

Timing: 20-40 days after filing

Duration: 5-10 minutes in most cases

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport)
  • Proof of Social Security number (SS card, W-2, or tax return)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 60 days)
  • Recent bank statements
  • Tax returns (most recent year)

Common Questions Asked

  • Did you review your petition before it was filed?
  • Is everything true and accurate?
  • Did you list all assets and debts?
  • Have you transferred any property in the last 2 years?
  • Do you own any real estate?
  • Are you expecting any inheritance or legal settlement?
  • Have you filed bankruptcy before?

Answer honestly and briefly. If you do not know, say so.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens at the 341 meeting?

The trustee asks about your finances under oath. It takes 5-10 minutes. Creditors can attend but rarely do.

Is a judge present?

No. The meeting is conducted by the trustee, not a judge. It is less formal than a courtroom hearing.

Can the meeting be continued?

Yes. If the trustee needs more documents, the meeting can be rescheduled.
