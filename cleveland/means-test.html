The Means Test for Cleveland Filers

The means test determines whether you qualify for Chapter 7 bankruptcy. It compares your income to the Ohio median.

How the Means Test Works

  1. Step 1 -- Income comparison: Calculate your average monthly income over the past 6 months and compare it to the Ohio median for your household size. If below, you pass.
  2. Step 2 -- Expense deductions: If your income is above the median, you deduct allowed expenses. If the remaining "disposable income" is low enough, you still qualify.

Income Excluded from the Means Test

  • Social Security benefits
  • Certain veterans' disability payments
  • Payments to victims of war crimes or terrorism

All other income counts -- wages, self-employment, rental income, pension, unemployment, and household contributions.

What If You Do Not Pass?

  • File Chapter 13: No means test required. Learn about Chapter 13.
  • Wait and refile: If your income recently dropped, waiting may change your 6-month average.
  • Special circumstances: Argue additional expense deductions for medical conditions or military duty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the means test?

A calculation comparing your income to Ohio's median. If below, you qualify for Chapter 7 automatically.

What if I fail?

File Chapter 13 (no means test), wait for your average to drop, or argue special circumstances.

Is Social Security counted?

No. Social Security benefits are excluded.
