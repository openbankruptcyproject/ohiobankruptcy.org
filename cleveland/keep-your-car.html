Keeping Your Car in Bankruptcy - Cleveland, Ohio

Worried about losing your car? Most Cleveland filers keep their vehicles. Here is how Ohio's exemptions and bankruptcy law protect your car.

Ohio's Vehicle Exemption

Ohio protects $4,060 of equity in one motor vehicle.

  • Car worth $8,000, owe $6,000: Equity $2,000 - fully protected
  • Car worth $15,000, owe $15,000: Equity $0 - fully protected
  • Car worth $12,000, owe nothing: Equity $12,000 - exceeds exemption

You can also apply the $1,350 wildcard exemption to protect additional vehicle equity.

Chapter 7: Reaffirmation and Redemption

Reaffirmation

Sign a new agreement to continue making payments. The loan survives bankruptcy and you keep the car. Most common option.

Redemption

Pay the lender the car's current market value in a lump sum, regardless of loan balance. Requires cash upfront.

Surrender

Return the car and discharge the remaining loan balance. An option if the car is not worth keeping.

Chapter 13: Cramdown

If you purchased your car more than 910 days before filing:

  • Loan balance reduced to the car's current market value
  • Interest rate may be reduced
  • Pay the reduced amount through your 3-5 year plan

Example: Owe $18,000, car worth $10,000. Pay $10,000 plus interest. The $8,000 difference becomes unsecured debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep my car?

Usually yes. Ohio's $4,060 exemption protects most cars. In Chapter 13 you always keep it.

What is a cramdown?

In Chapter 13, the loan is reduced to the car's value if purchased 910+ days before filing.

What is reaffirmation?

A new agreement in Chapter 7 to keep paying your car loan. The debt survives discharge.
