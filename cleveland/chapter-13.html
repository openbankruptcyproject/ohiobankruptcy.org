Chapter 13 Bankruptcy in Cleveland, Ohio

Chapter 13 lets you keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. It is often the best option for Cleveland homeowners facing foreclosure or car repossession.

What Is Chapter 13 Bankruptcy?

Chapter 13 is a reorganization bankruptcy. Instead of liquidating assets, you propose a repayment plan to pay back some or all of your debts over 3-5 years. You make monthly payments to a Chapter 13 trustee, who distributes the funds to your creditors.

Chapter 13 is especially powerful for:

  • Stopping foreclosure and catching up on your mortgage
  • Preventing car repossession
  • Paying back taxes over time
  • Protecting co-signers from creditor action

Chapter 13 Plan Length

  • Below median income: 3-year plan (36 months)
  • Above median income: 5-year plan (60 months)

Your monthly payment is calculated based on your disposable income -- the amount left after reasonable living expenses.

Saving Your Home in Cleveland

If you are behind on your mortgage, Chapter 13 can stop foreclosure immediately through the automatic stay. Your repayment plan lets you catch up on missed payments over the life of the plan while continuing regular mortgage payments.

Ohio's $145,425 homestead exemption protects your equity. In Chapter 13, you keep your home regardless of equity -- you just need to pay unsecured creditors at least as much as they would receive in a Chapter 7 liquidation.

Keeping Your Car in Cleveland

Chapter 13 can stop repossession and let you catch up on missed car payments through your plan. If you purchased your car more than 910 days before filing, you may be able to reduce the loan balance to the car's current value (called a "cramdown").

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Chapter 13 work in Cleveland?

You propose a 3-5 year repayment plan. A trustee collects your monthly payment and distributes it to creditors. You keep all your property while repaying debts.

Can I save my house from foreclosure?

Yes. Filing Chapter 13 stops foreclosure immediately. You catch up on missed payments through your plan. Learn more.

How long is a Chapter 13 plan?

3 years if below Ohio median income, 5 years if above.

What if I cannot make my plan payments?

You can request a plan modification if circumstances change. In some cases, you may convert to Chapter 7 or seek a hardship discharge.
