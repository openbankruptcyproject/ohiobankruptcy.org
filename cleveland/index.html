Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 in Cleveland

Chapter 7 - Fresh Start

Eliminates most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills) in about 3-4 months. Must pass a means test based on Ohio median income.

  • Discharge in 3-4 months
  • Eliminates credit cards, medical debt
  • Must pass the means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Filing fee: $338

Chapter 13 - Payment Plan

Keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for saving a home from foreclosure or a car from repossession.

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep your home and car
  • Catch up on missed payments
  • No means test requirement
  • Filing fee: $313

Cost of Filing Bankruptcy in Cleveland

  • Chapter 7 filing fee: $338 (installment payments available)
  • Chapter 13 filing fee: $313 (can be paid through your plan)
  • Attorney fees: Typically $1,000-$1,800 for Chapter 7; $2,500-$4,000 for Chapter 13
  • Credit counseling: $15-25 per course (two required)

Fee waivers are available for Chapter 7 filers whose income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines.

Free Legal Help in Cleveland

Legal Aid Society of Cleveland

The Legal Aid Society of Cleveland provides free legal assistance to qualifying low-income residents in Cuyahoga County and surrounding areas. They can help with bankruptcy questions, creditor harassment, and debt collection defense.

If you cannot afford an attorney, contact Legal Aid to see if you qualify for free representation or guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions - Cleveland Bankruptcy

Where do I file bankruptcy in Cleveland?

Cleveland residents file in the Northern District of Ohio. The courthouse is at 801 W Superior Ave, Cleveland, OH 44113. You file in this district if you have lived in northern Ohio for the greater part of the last 180 days.

How much does bankruptcy cost in Cleveland?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees in Cleveland typically range from $1,000 to $1,800 for Chapter 7 and $2,500 to $4,000 for Chapter 13. Full cost breakdown.

Can I keep my house in Cleveland if I file bankruptcy?

Ohio's homestead exemption protects up to $145,425 of equity in your home. If your equity is below that amount, you can keep your home in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you keep your home while catching up on missed payments. Learn more.

Is there free legal help for bankruptcy in Cleveland?

Yes. The Legal Aid Society of Cleveland provides free legal assistance to qualifying low-income residents. Contact them to see if you qualify for help with your bankruptcy case.

How long does bankruptcy take in Cleveland?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months from filing to discharge. Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan. Your 341 meeting of creditors usually happens about 30 days after filing at the Cleveland courthouse.
