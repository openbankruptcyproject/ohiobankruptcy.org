Bankruptcy Exemptions for Cleveland Filers

Ohio's exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. Cleveland residents can choose between Ohio state exemptions and federal exemptions.

State vs. Federal Exemptions

Ohio allows you to choose between state and federal exemptions. You must use one complete set -- you cannot mix and match.

When state exemptions may be better: You have significant home equity (state homestead is $145,425 vs. federal $27,900).

When federal exemptions may be better: You do not own a home and want to use the federal wildcard to protect other property.

Consult a qualified attorney to determine which set is best for your situation.

How Exemptions Work

  • Chapter 7: The trustee can only take property that exceeds your exemption amounts. If all your property is exempt, you keep everything.
  • Chapter 13: You keep all property, but your plan must pay unsecured creditors at least as much as they would have received in a Chapter 7 liquidation of non-exempt assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the homestead exemption in Ohio?

$145,425 of equity in your primary residence. Doubles for joint filers.

Can I choose federal exemptions?

Yes. Ohio lets you choose state or federal, but you must use one set entirely.

How much vehicle equity can I protect?

$4,060 for one motor vehicle under Ohio state exemptions.

What is the wildcard exemption?

$1,350 that can be applied to any property not covered by other exemptions.
