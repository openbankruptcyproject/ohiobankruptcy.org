Bankruptcy FAQ -- Cleveland, Ohio

Answers to the most common questions Cleveland residents have about filing bankruptcy in the Northern District of Ohio.

General Questions

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months but requires passing a means test. Chapter 13 creates a 3-5 year repayment plan. Compare chapters.

Where do I file in Cleveland?

Northern District of Ohio. Courthouse: 801 W Superior Ave, Cleveland, OH 44113. ohnb.uscourts.gov.

How much does it cost?

Chapter 7: $338 filing fee + $1,000-$1,800 attorney. Chapter 13: $313 + $2,500-$4,000. Full cost breakdown.

How long does bankruptcy take?

Chapter 7: 3-4 months. Chapter 13: 3-5 years.

Will bankruptcy ruin my credit?

Bankruptcy stays on your credit report for 7-10 years. But many people see improvement within 1-2 years. Rebuilding credit.

Property Questions

Can I keep my house?

Ohio's $145,425 homestead exemption protects most homes. In Chapter 13, you keep your home while catching up. Full guide.

Can I keep my car?

Ohio protects $4,060 of vehicle equity. If you owe more than the car's value, equity is zero and it is fully protected. Full guide.

What about retirement accounts?

Fully exempt. 401k, IRA, and pension cannot be taken by the trustee.

What about my tax refund?

Tax refunds are assets. Use Ohio's $1,350 wildcard exemption to protect some of it.

Process Questions

What happens at the 341 meeting?

The trustee asks about your finances under oath. It lasts 5-10 minutes. Full guide.

Can I file without an attorney?

Yes, but mistakes can be costly. The Legal Aid Society of Cleveland may offer free help.

Can I file bankruptcy again?

Yes, with timing restrictions. Check your eligibility. Learn more.

What debts survive bankruptcy?

Student loans (usually), recent taxes, child support, alimony, government fines, and debts from fraud or DUI.
